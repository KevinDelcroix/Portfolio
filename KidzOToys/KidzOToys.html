What's the game about ?

SYou play as two mega-robots powered by the imagination of two children. Collaborate to find your way through a toy-filled world to reach the magnificent castle.

Use your weapons, the "maxitoys", which will evolve in wacky ways throughout the adventure to defeat your enemies, the evil toy-soldiers. Explore a miniature world where you can make your weapons evolve until they become overpowered, and help each other to defeat waves of enemies. The game is a twin-stick shooter, designed for PC with a two-player co-op mode. It features a top-down shared view and can be played with a gamepad.

My Work on the Project

Kidz’o Toys - Master’s Project Prototype

This game was developed as part of my master's degree in game development. We had one week to produce a game prototype as a team, with the primary goal being to establish a strong bond within the team that would accompany us throughout our 5th year of studies.

From the very beginning, we decided to create a twin-stick shooter set in a childlike universe. This choice of genre was a way to bring fun mechanics to a colorful and playful world.

My Role as Gameplay Programmer

As a gameplay programmer, I was responsible for implementing various key features. One of the most notable mechanics I worked on was the double lever doors: these doors require both players to interact with separate levers simultaneously within a short time frame to open. This mechanic was designed to encourage teamwork, as both players must coordinate and act quickly.

I also implemented the pressure plates, which are only activated when the first plate is triggered, revealing the next one. This system also encourages collaboration, as one player must remain on the plate while the other progresses, creating moments of strategy and cooperation.

Additionally, I worked on the integration of the game's various weapons. Each weapon has its own unique usage mechanics, and I ensured they were properly balanced and functional within the game world. The weapons evolve throughout the adventure, adding complexity and excitement as players progress.

A fun addition to the game is the Soda Trap (formerly known as the "Coke Trap"). This is a spilled soda that creates a slippery hazard on the ground, slowing down player movement when they step into it. Since the game is set in a toy world and features cola as part of the environment, it felt fitting to have a playful, yet challenging obstacle like this to add variety to the gameplay. Players must be careful not to get stuck in these traps while navigating the toy world.

Key Technical Details

  • Double Lever Doors: This feature required a timed synchronization system. I implemented a timer-based mechanic that checks whether both players press their respective levers within a specific window of time. If successful, the door opens; if not, it remains closed. This required precise input handling and state synchronization for each player.
  • Pressure Plates: The pressure plate mechanic was designed using a trigger system. I programmed the plates to remain inactive unless the first one was activated, using a state-machine system that managed the progression. This ensures that the level design flows smoothly, with players needing to work together to proceed.
  • Soda Trap: The Soda Trap slows down the player who steps into the spilled cola by applying a deceleration force on the player’s movement. This was achieved by creating a specific collision detection system that triggers the slow-down effect upon contact. It adds a unique environmental hazard and requires players to time their movements carefully to avoid being caught in the trap.
  • Weapon Integration: For the weapons, I worked on the item management system to ensure smooth integration of different weapon types. Each weapon has a unique set of attributes (damage, range, etc.), and I implemented a dynamic system where weapons evolve and become more powerful over time. This involved using object pooling for efficiency and ensuring proper weapon swapping mechanics during gameplay.

Conclusion

This week was a great opportunity for me to demonstrate my programming skills and to grow as a team player. I was able to contribute technically while also learning from my colleagues' expertise. It laid the foundation for the collaborative work we'll continue throughout the rest of our studies.
